Welcome to Kitchen Comebacks

Welcome to Kitchen Comebacks, a podcast by Porkchop where we explore the profound healing journey of trauma survivors who found solace, restoration, and self-worth in the kitchen. This isn't about simple challenges - it's about deep wounds from childhood abuse, abandonment, betrayal, and loss, and how the meditative practice of cooking becomes both sanctuary and path forward.

Sometimes, it's a grandfather or mother who creates those rare moments of safety and connection through cooking - teaching not just recipes, but modeling what healthy relationships can look like. These kitchen sanctuaries become lifelines that sustain us through darkness. Each episode honors authentic stories of resilience and intergenerational healing that go beyond "overcoming" to true restoration.

Latest Episodes

Episode 1 - When the Kitchen Becomes Sanctuary

In our premiere episode, we explore how creating meals with our hands can restore what was taken by trauma. For survivors of childhood abuse and neglect, the simple act of cooking alongside a supportive figure - a grandparent, parent, or mentor - can provide islands of safety in a turbulent childhood. Years later, these kitchen moments often become the foundation for healing, as the kitchen transforms from a place of exploitation into a sanctuary where control, creativity, and nurturing come together to rebuild what was broken.
